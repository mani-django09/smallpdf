What's the difference between JPG and PNG output formats?

JPG and PNG are both popular image formats, but they have different characteristics:

  • JPG format: Creates smaller file sizes through compression, which makes it ideal for photographs and images where slight quality reduction is acceptable. JPG doesn't support transparency.
  • PNG format: Offers lossless compression, maintaining higher image quality but with larger file sizes. PNG supports transparency and is better for images with text, line drawings, or graphics that require crisp edges.

We recommend JPG for most general purposes due to its universal compatibility and good balance between quality and file size. Choose PNG when you need the highest possible quality or when transparency is required.
